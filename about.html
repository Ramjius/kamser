Objectives
  • Advocate for widows' safety, property rights, and social justice.
  • Create resilience and sovereignty of their food systems, livelihoods, and wellbeing through sustainable agriculture and livelihood.
  • Addressing Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH), environmental conservation, disaster management, and climate change.
  • Improve governance for inclusive and sustainable peace and security.
  • Providing youth-friendly programs to foster vocational, Biblical entrepreneurial skills and talent development.
  • Appropriate housing with green energy sources.
  • Advocate for the provision of healthcare and psychosocial support for mental health.

Organization Profile

Name and Location:

KSWCSI, registered by the Ministry of Gender, Children and Social Development, is headquartered in Homabay County, specifically in Rachuonyo North Sub-County, Kendu-Bay Town. Correspondence can be directed to P.O. Box 210, Kendu Bay, with email contact at kscsi@gmail.com.

Nature of Organization:

KSWCSI is a not-for-profit, non-religious, and non-discriminatory entity, committed to serving individuals regardless of gender, race, or ethnicity. KSWCSI stands as a beacon of hope and empowerment, striving towards a society where all individuals, especially women and youth, have the resources and opportunities to thrive. Through its multifaceted approach encompassing advocacy, development programs, and community support, KSWCSI is making significant strides towards achieving its vision of a self-sustained society within an enabled environment.

Our Vision

"To be a self - sustained society with an enabled Environment"

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower widows and youth from social marginalization, isolation, and discrimination through sustainable agriculture, income-generating initiatives and entrepreneurial skills to enhance self-sustenance while preserving and enhancing the biodiversity of the ecosystem.
